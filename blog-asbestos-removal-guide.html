The Complete Guide to Asbestos Removal in Queensland 2024

By DL Demolition Team | Published: December 15, 2024 | 8 min read

Navigating the complexities of asbestos removal can be a daunting task for any property owner in Queensland. With strict regulations, significant health risks, and a myriad of technical details, understanding your legal obligations and ensuring the safety of your family or employees is paramount. This comprehensive guide provides everything you need to know about safe and compliant asbestos removal in the Sunshine State, from identifying potential hazards to understanding the costs involved.

Asbestos was a widely used building material in Australia for much of the 20th century, prized for its durability, fire resistance, and insulating properties. However, the inhalation of asbestos fibres is now known to cause serious and fatal diseases, including asbestosis, lung cancer, and mesothelioma [1]. For this reason, the Australian government has implemented stringent regulations to manage and remove asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) from the built environment.

Understanding Asbestos: Types and Dangers

Asbestos is a naturally occurring mineral fibre that is categorised into two main groups: friable and non-friable (or bonded) asbestos.

  • Friable Asbestos: This type of asbestos can be crumbled, pulverised, or reduced to powder by hand pressure when dry. It is the most dangerous form as the fibres are more easily released into the air. Examples include thermal insulation, insulation boards, and pipe lagging.
  • Non-Friable (Bonded) Asbestos: In this form, the asbestos fibres are mixed with a bonding agent, such as cement or resin, and cannot be easily disturbed. It is considered less of a risk than friable asbestos, but can become friable if damaged, weathered, or worked on. Common examples include asbestos cement sheeting, vinyl floor tiles, and roofing materials.

It is crucial to treat all suspected asbestos with the utmost caution. The only way to be certain if a material contains asbestos is to have it tested by a NATA-accredited laboratory.

Legal Framework for Asbestos Removal in Queensland

In Queensland, the management and removal of asbestos are governed by the Work Health and Safety Act 2011 and the Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011. The How to Safely Remove Asbestos Code of Practice 2021 provides practical guidance on how to comply with these laws [2].

Key Regulatory Requirements

Requirement Description
Licensing A licence is required to remove more than 10 square metres of non-friable asbestos or any amount of friable asbestos.
Training Workers and supervisors must be appropriately trained and hold specific certifications.
Notification WorkSafe Queensland must be notified at least five days before licensed asbestos removal work commences.
Air Monitoring Required for all friable asbestos removal work to ensure fibre levels remain within safe limits.
Clearance Certificate An independent licensed asbestos assessor must issue a clearance certificate before a work area can be reoccupied after friable asbestos removal.
Waste Disposal Asbestos waste must be sealed, labelled, and transported to a licensed disposal facility.

Homeowners are permitted to remove up to 10 square metres of non-friable asbestos themselves, provided they hold a certificate from a recognised training course [3]. However, due to the significant health risks, it is always recommended to engage a licensed professional.

The Asbestos Removal Process: A Step-by-Step Guide

A professional asbestos removal project follows a strict set of procedures to ensure safety and compliance.

  1. Initial Assessment and Testing: A licensed professional will inspect your property to identify potential ACMs and take samples for laboratory testing.
  2. Asbestos Removal Control Plan (ARCP): A detailed plan is developed outlining the scope of work, risk control measures, and emergency procedures.
  3. Site Preparation: The work area is isolated and sealed off with heavy-duty plastic sheeting to prevent the spread of fibres. Warning signs are erected at all entry points.
  4. Removal: Trained professionals wearing personal protective equipment (PPE) carefully remove the ACMs using specialised tools and techniques to minimise fibre release.
  5. Decontamination: The work area is thoroughly cleaned using HEPA-filtered vacuum cleaners and wet-wiping methods. All asbestos waste is sealed in labelled, heavy-duty bags or plastic.
  6. Clearance Inspection: For friable asbestos removal, an independent licensed asbestos assessor conducts a visual inspection and air monitoring to ensure the area is safe for reoccupation.
  7. Waste Disposal: The sealed asbestos waste is transported to a licensed disposal facility in accordance with environmental regulations.

Choosing the Right Asbestos Removal Professional

Selecting a qualified and reputable asbestos removalist is the most critical decision you will make in this process. Here are some key factors to consider:

  • Licensing: Verify that the company holds the appropriate class of asbestos removal licence (Class A for friable, Class B for non-friable) from WorkSafe Queensland.
  • Experience: Look for a company with a proven track record and experience in projects similar to yours.
  • Insurance: Ensure the company has adequate public liability and asbestos-specific insurance.
  • References: Ask for references from previous clients and check online reviews.
  • Detailed Quote: The quote should be comprehensive and include all aspects of the job, from site preparation to waste disposal and clearance certification.

The Cost of Asbestos Removal in Queensland

The cost of asbestos removal can vary significantly depending on several factors, including:

  • The type and quantity of asbestos
  • The location and accessibility of the ACMs
  • The complexity of the removal process
  • The need for air monitoring and a clearance certificate

As a general guide, you can expect to pay:

Non-Friable Asbestos Removal $30 - $50 per square metre

These prices are indicative only. Always obtain a detailed, written quote from a licensed professional before commencing any work.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Can I dispose of asbestos in my regular bin?

A: Absolutely not. Asbestos waste must be sealed, labelled, and taken to a facility that is licensed to accept it. Illegal dumping of asbestos carries severe penalties.

Q: What should I do if I accidentally damage material that might contain asbestos?

A: Evacuate the area immediately, close it off to prevent access, and contact a licensed asbestos professional for advice. Do not attempt to clean it up yourself.

Q: How long does asbestos removal take?

A: The duration depends on the size and complexity of the job. A small residential project might take a day, while a large commercial project could take several weeks.

Conclusion

Asbestos removal is a serious undertaking that should not be taken lightly. By understanding the risks, adhering to the legal requirements, and engaging qualified professionals, you can ensure the process is completed safely and effectively, protecting the health of your family, employees, and the wider community.
