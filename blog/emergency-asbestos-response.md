# Emergency Asbestos Response: What to Do When You Find Asbestos

**Discovering suspected asbestos in your home or workplace is a serious situation that requires immediate and correct action. Panic is a natural reaction, but a calm, methodical approach is crucial to ensure everyone's safety. This guide outlines the essential steps to take, who to call, and what to expect from a professional emergency asbestos response.**

---

## 1. Immediate Steps: Safety First

If you accidentally disturb materials you suspect contain asbestos (e.g., by drilling, sanding, or breaking them), the priority is to minimize fiber release and exposure.

**Do's:**
- **Stop all activity immediately.**
- **Evacuate the area.** Keep everyone, especially children and pets, away from the contaminated zone.
- **Close all doors and windows** leading to the area to prevent drafts from spreading fibers.
- **Turn off any air conditioning, heating, or ventilation systems** that service the area.
- **Place warning signs** to prevent others from entering.

**Don'ts:**
- **Do not attempt to clean up the debris yourself.** Sweeping, vacuuming, or using a standard dustpan will make asbestos fibers airborne.
- **Do not touch or move the material.**
- **Do not eat, drink, or smoke** in or near the contaminated area.

> **Important:** Asbestos fibers are microscopic and can remain airborne for days. Even if you can't see any dust, the danger is still present.

## 3. The Professional Emergency Response Process

When our emergency team arrives, they will follow a strict, regulated protocol to secure the site and eliminate the hazard.

**Step 1: Site Assessment and Isolation**
- The team will assess the extent of the contamination.
- The area will be completely sealed off using heavy-duty plastic sheeting and tape.
- Negative air pressure units may be installed to ensure contaminated air cannot escape the work zone.

**Step 2: Decontamination and Removal**
- Technicians in full PPE will enter the sealed area.
- All visible debris is wetted down to suppress dust and carefully collected.
- The area is meticulously cleaned using H-type vacuums designed to capture asbestos fibers.
- All contaminated materials are double-bagged in labeled, heavy-duty asbestos waste bags.

**Step 3: Air Monitoring and Clearance**
- After the cleanup, an independent licensed asbestos assessor will conduct a visual inspection and air monitoring.
- Air samples are taken and analyzed to ensure the fiber count is below the safety threshold (0.01 fibres/mL).
- Only after the air is deemed safe will a **Clearance Certificate** be issued.

**Step 4: Safe and Legal Disposal**
- All sealed asbestos waste is transported by a licensed carrier to an approved waste facility.
- We provide you with waste disposal receipts as proof of legal and safe disposal.

## 4. What NOT to Do: Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Using a Household Vacuum:** Standard vacuums do not have filters fine enough to capture asbestos fibers. They will simply spread the contamination throughout your property.
- **Washing Contaminated Clothing:** Do not wash clothes worn during the exposure with other laundry. They must be disposed of as asbestos waste.
- **Ignoring the Problem:** Hoping the dust will settle is not a solution. The fibers will remain a hazard until professionally removed.
