# Cost Factors in Professional Demolition: A Gold Coast & Brisbane Guide

**Planning a demolition project is a significant undertaking, and one of the first questions homeowners and developers ask is: "How much will it cost?" The answer is complex, as demolition costs are influenced by a wide range of factors. This guide breaks down the key variables that determine the price of a professional demolition project in the Gold Coast and Brisbane regions.**

---

## 1. Project Size and Scope

The most direct cost factor is the size of the structure to be demolished. A larger house or commercial building requires more labour, more machine time, and generates more waste, all of which increase the cost.

- **Full Demolition:** Complete teardown of a structure to ground level.
- **Partial Demolition / Strip-Out:** Removal of specific internal or external sections (e.g., a kitchen, bathroom, or a single wall). This is often more complex and can have a higher per-square-meter cost due to the need for precision and protection of remaining structures.

## 2. Asbestos Presence and Removal

This is the single most significant variable that can impact the cost. If asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) are present, the project is no longer just a demolition; it is a hazardous material removal project that must adhere to strict safety and regulatory standards.

| Factor | Impact on Cost |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asbestos Identification** | Cost of a professional asbestos survey and testing. |
| **Type of Asbestos** | Friable (e.g., insulation) is more expensive to remove than non-friable (e.g., cement sheeting). |
| **Quantity and Location** | More asbestos, or asbestos in difficult-to-access areas (like roof spaces), increases labour and time. |
| **Safety Measures** | Cost of full site encapsulation, negative air units, PPE, and air monitoring. |
| **Disposal Fees** | Asbestos waste disposal fees are significantly higher than standard construction waste. |

> **Warning:** Never cut corners on asbestos removal. The long-term health costs far outweigh any short-term savings. Always use a Class A or B licensed professional.

## 3. Site Accessibility

How easily can heavy machinery access your site? A property on a flat, open block is much cheaper to demolish than one on a steep, narrow, or difficult-to-access site.

- **Narrow Access:** Requires smaller, less efficient machinery, increasing time and labour.
- **Steep Slopes:** May require specialized equipment and additional safety precautions.
- **Proximity to Neighbours:** Tight spaces require more careful, slower work to prevent damage to adjacent properties, increasing costs.

## 4. Waste Management and Recycling

Disposal is a major component of demolition costs. The type and volume of materials to be removed will dictate the price.

- **Mixed Debris:** Cheaper to dispose of but less environmentally friendly.
- **Source Separation:** Separating materials like concrete, timber, and metal for recycling can sometimes reduce disposal fees, but increases labour costs. At DL Demolition, we prioritize recycling to minimize environmental impact.
- **Hazardous Materials:** Besides asbestos, other materials like lead paint, PCBs, or contaminated soil require special (and more expensive) handling and disposal.

## 5. Required Permits and Council Fees

Demolition in Queensland requires council approval. The costs can include:

- **Application Fees:** Paid to the local council (e.g., Gold Coast City Council, Brisbane City Council).
- **Traffic Management Plans:** If the demolition impacts public roads or footpaths.
- **Service Disconnections:** Fees for safely disconnecting electricity, water, gas, and telecommunications.

## Example Cost Breakdown (Hypothetical)

For a standard 3-bedroom brick house on a flat block in the Gold Coast with no asbestos:

| Item | Estimated Cost (AUD) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Labour & Machinery | $8,000 - $12,000 |
| Waste Disposal (150-200 tonnes) | $6,000 - $9,000 |
| Permits & Fees | $1,000 - $2,000 |
| **Total (No Asbestos)** | **$15,000 - $23,000** |

Now, the same house **with asbestos** in the roof, eaves, and internal walls:

| Item | Estimated Cost (AUD) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Base Demolition Cost | $15,000 - $23,000 |
| Asbestos Removal (Licensed) | $7,000 - $15,000+ |
| **Total (With Asbestos)** | **$22,000 - $38,000+** |

*These are estimates only. Every project is unique.*
