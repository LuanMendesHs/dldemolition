># Signs Your Roof Contains Asbestos: What Gold Coast Homeowners Need to Know

**By DL Demolition Team** | Published: December 10, 2024 | 5 min read

---

For many homeowners on the Gold Coast, the thought of asbestos lurking in their property is a significant concern. Given that Australia was one of the highest per capita users of asbestos in the world, it's a valid worry, especially for homes built before 1990. The roof is one of the most common places to find asbestos-containing materials (ACMs), and understanding the potential signs can be the first step in ensuring your family's safety.

This article will guide you through the common indicators that your roof may contain asbestos, the types of roofing materials to be aware of, and the crucial steps to take if you suspect its presence.

> **Important Note:** The only way to be 100% certain if a material contains asbestos is through professional testing. This guide is for informational purposes only and should not replace a professional assessment.

## The Age of Your Home: The First Red Flag

The most significant indicator of asbestos in your home is its construction date. If your house was built or renovated:

*   **Before the mid-1980s:** It is **highly likely** to contain asbestos.
*   **Between the mid-1980s and 1990:** It is **likely** to contain asbestos.
*   **After 1990:** It is **unlikely** to contain asbestos, as a partial ban was introduced.
*   **After 2003:** It is **very unlikely** to contain asbestos, as a total ban was implemented.

If your property falls into the first two categories, a professional inspection is strongly recommended, regardless of the appearance of your roofing materials.

## Common Asbestos Roofing Materials

Asbestos was used in a variety of roofing products due to its strength, durability, and fire-resistant properties. Here are the most common types found in Queensland homes:

### 1. Corrugated Asbestos Cement Sheeting

This is perhaps the most recognisable form of asbestos roofing. It looks like typical corrugated iron or steel sheeting but is made from asbestos cement.

**How to Identify It:**

*   **Appearance:** Large, grey, corrugated sheets. They are often thicker and more brittle than modern metal roofing.
*   **Texture:** The surface may have a fibrous or dimpled texture, especially when weathered.
*   **Markings:** Some sheets may have manufacturer's marks or codes, but these can be hard to find or decipher.

### 2. Asbestos Roof Tiles or Shingles

Asbestos was also used to create roof tiles or shingles, which can be harder to distinguish from their non-asbestos counterparts.

**How to Identify Them:**

*   **Appearance:** They often resemble slate or concrete tiles and can be of various shapes and sizes.
*   **Colour:** While they can be painted, the underlying material is typically a uniform grey or white.
*   **Brittleness:** Asbestos tiles are known to be very brittle and can crack or break easily under pressure.

### 3. Asbestos Roofing Felt

Used as an underlayment for other roofing materials, asbestos-containing felt provided an extra layer of insulation and fire protection.

**How to Identify It:**

*   **Location:** Found beneath the primary roofing material (e.g., tiles or shingles).
*   **Appearance:** A black, tar-like paper that may have a fibrous texture.

## Visual Signs of Potential Asbestos

While visual identification is not definitive, there are some characteristics that can suggest the presence of asbestos:

| Sign | Description |
|---|---|
| **Dimpled or Pitted Surface** | Weathered asbestos cement sheets often develop a pattern of small dimples or pits. |
| **White or Grey Colour** | The natural colour of asbestos cement is a light grey or white. If the paint is peeling, this underlying colour may be visible. |
| **Brittleness and Cracking** | Asbestos materials become very brittle with age and may show signs of cracking, chipping, or breaking. |
| **Lack of Metallic Sheen** | Unlike metal roofing, asbestos cement will not have a metallic lustre, even when new. |

## What to Do if You Suspect Asbestos

If you have any reason to believe your roof may contain asbestos, it is crucial to act with caution.

1.  **Do Not Disturb It:** Avoid any activities that could damage the material, such as walking on the roof, drilling, cutting, or high-pressure cleaning.
2.  **Assume It Is Asbestos:** Until proven otherwise by a professional, treat the material as if it contains asbestos.
3.  **Contact a Licensed Professional:** Engage a licensed asbestos assessor or removalist to inspect the roof. They can take a sample for testing in a NATA-accredited laboratory.
4.  **Plan for Removal:** If the presence of asbestos is confirmed and the material is in poor condition, you will need to arrange for its removal by a licensed professional. In Queensland, a licence is required to remove more than 10 square metres of non-friable asbestos [1].

## The Dangers of DIY Asbestos Removal

While it may be tempting to save money by removing a small amount of asbestos yourself, the risks are significant. Improper removal can release dangerous fibres into the air, contaminating your property and endangering the health of your family and neighbours. Licensed professionals have the training, equipment, and experience to remove asbestos safely and in accordance with strict regulations.

## Conclusion

Identifying potential asbestos in your roof is the first step towards creating a safe environment for your family. While the age of your home and the appearance of your roofing materials can provide clues, the only way to be certain is through professional testing. If you have any concerns about asbestos in your Gold Coast property, don't hesitate to contact a licensed professional for an assessment. The peace of mind is well worth the investment.
