# How to Choose the Right Demolition Contractor: A 10-Point Checklist

**Choosing a demolition contractor is one of the most critical decisions you'll make for your construction or renovation project. The right contractor ensures the job is done safely, on time, and on budget. The wrong one can lead to costly delays, safety hazards, and legal nightmares. This 10-point checklist will help you navigate the process and select a top-tier professional in the Gold Coast and Brisbane region.**

---

## 1. Verify Licensing and Insurance (Non-Negotiable)

This is the most important step. A legitimate contractor must have the correct licenses and insurance.

- **License:** In Queensland, a contractor must hold a **Demolition Licence** from the Queensland Building and Construction Commission (QBCC). For any asbestos work, they must also hold a **Class A or Class B Asbestos Removal Licence**.
- **Insurance:** They must have current **Public Liability Insurance** (minimum $20 million is standard) to cover any accidental damage to your or your neighbours' property.

> **Action:** Ask for copies of their license and Certificate of Currency for their insurance. Verify the license on the [QBCC website](https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/).

## 2. Check Their Safety Record

Demolition is inherently dangerous. A contractor's safety record is a direct reflection of their professionalism.

- **Ask about their safety processes.** Do they develop a **Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS)** for every project?
- **Inquire about their accident history.** A reputable company will be transparent about this.

## 3. Experience and Specialization

Not all demolition is the same. Does the contractor have experience with your specific type of project?

- **Residential vs. Commercial:** The equipment and processes can differ significantly.
- **Partial vs. Full Demolition:** Internal strip-outs require a delicate touch, while full demolitions require heavy machinery expertise.
- **Asbestos Expertise:** If your property was built before 1990, choose a contractor who specializes in asbestos removal, not one who just subcontracts it out.

## 4. Get Detailed, Written Quotes

Never accept a verbal quote. A professional quote should be a detailed document that breaks down all costs.

**A good quote includes:**
- Scope of work
- Cost of labour and machinery
- Estimated cost of waste disposal
- Cost of permits and fees
- Timeline for the project
- Payment terms

> **Action:** Get at least three quotes from different licensed contractors to compare. Be wary of a quote that is significantly lower than the others; they may be cutting corners on safety or disposal.

## 5. Ask for References and Check Reviews

Past performance is a strong indicator of future results.

- **Ask for references** from recent, similar projects and actually call them.
- **Check online reviews** on Google, Facebook, and industry-specific sites. Look for patterns in the feedback.

## 6. Understand Their Waste Management Process

How a contractor handles waste says a lot about their professionalism and environmental responsibility.

- **Ask about their recycling policy.** Do they separate materials like concrete, metal, and timber?
- **For asbestos, demand proof of legal disposal.** They should be able to provide you with receipts from a licensed waste facility.

## 7. Evaluate Their Communication and Professionalism

From the first phone call, are they professional, responsive, and easy to communicate with? If they are hard to get a hold of for a quote, imagine how it will be once they have your money.

## 8. Review the Contract Carefully

Before signing anything, read the contract thoroughly. Ensure it matches the quote and includes all the details you discussed. Pay close attention to clauses about unforeseen circumstances and how variations to the project will be handled.

## 9. Assess Their Equipment

Does the contractor own their own equipment, or do they rent it? While renting isn't necessarily a red flag, contractors who own and maintain their own fleet (like DL Demolition) often have better availability and more control over the project timeline.

## 10. Trust Your Gut

After all the due diligence, do you feel comfortable and confident with this contractor? If something feels off, it probably is. Choose the contractor who you trust to do the job safely and professionally.
